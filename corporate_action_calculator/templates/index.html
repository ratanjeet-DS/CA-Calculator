FNO Regulatory Corporate Action Adjustments

Official exchange methodologies, circulars, and formulas utilized to compute derivatives contracts restructuring events.

When bonus shares are issued, shareholders receive additional shares without incurring cash expenses. This increases total shared volume outstanding and decreases market value per share proportionally.

Adjustment Factor formula
Adjustment Factor = (Bonus Ratio Numerator + Bonus Ratio Denominator) / Bonus Ratio Denominator
Options Striking Prices: Divided by adjustment factor and rounded off to nearest tick of ₹0.05.
Futures Base Rates: Divided by adjustment factor and rounded according to futures tick formatting.
FNO Lot Size: Multiplied by adjustment factor so total outstanding base risk is maintained.

A stock split is designed to partition individual shares, changing total floats. Stock splitting changes face/par values, requiring proportional FNO contract splits.

Formula parameters
New Strike = Old Strike / Split Ratio
New Futures = Old Futures / Split Ratio
New Lot = Old Lot * Split Ratio

Adjusted option premium contract values remain financially neutral before and after the ex-split date.

Under SEBI and derivative guidelines, dividend adjustments rely entirely on dividend size percentages compared to the market closing stock rate:

  • Regular Dividend: Payout represents less than 5% of share pricing. No adjustments are done.
  • Extraordinary Dividend: Dividend equals or exceeds 5% of pre-event price.
Adjusment Rules
Adjusted Strike Price = Strike Price - Dividend Amount
Adjusted Futures Price = Settlement Price - Dividend Amount

Rights distributions require evaluating ex-rights pricing variables (Theoretical Ex-Rights Price, TERP) to balance discount incentives offered to current corporate share investors.

Computational Steps
TERP = [(Existing Shares × Cum Rights Price) + (Rights Shares × Rights Issue Price)] / (Existing + Rights Shares)
Factor = TERP / Cum-Rights Price

Option contract premiums adjust by multiplying striking thresholds by the factor, while sizing variables shift conversely.
